Barrier Reef Orchestra

About Us

The Townsville Barrier Reef Orchestra was has been around since the year 2000. In the years before it’s formation, many Townsville art and music lovers sought to introduce a community based orchestra into their city’s culture. When the new millennium came around, so too did the opportunity they’d waited for.

To test the viability of this new orchestra, a weekend workshop began that was run by the senior lecturer of the Canberra School of Music – Richard McIntyre. Many aspiring musicians were invited to these meetings, and thus the Barrier Reef Orchestra was born.

Since then, the orchestra has performed many times with various guest conductors directing the show under the patronage of the North Queensland Ensembles Inc. While typically the orchestra as a whole only performs 3 major concerts per year, smaller groups of orchestra members regularly perform throughout the year in shows such as Orpheus Strings, Pandanus Winds, Kingfisher Trio and many more.

Orchestra Composition

Music is the culmination of a variety of sounds combined in such a way that inspires feelings in the listener. Orchestras use this to a greater degree, which allows for more nuances in the sound created.

With so many different tones and pitches, it can be difficult to identify which instrument is responsible for what. So, for those looking to find out more, below is a compilation of an orchestra’s many families of sound and the instruments that belong to them.

String – String instruments produce theirs sounds by vibrating the strings. By changing the thickness, length, and tension of the string, the sound created can be modified. Strings that are tight or thin create a higher pitched sound compared to strings that are loose or thick. Some string instruments also have companion tools that utilize their own strings in harmony with the main instrument.

Brass – Brass instruments are the tubed shaped instruments that players use with their lips. Instruments with larger mouth piece openings produce lower base sounds than those with small mouth pieces. The main method of altering a brass instruments sound is by opening or closing tubes of various length via valves. As tubes of different length create different sounds, the sounds each of these tubes make will be different to each adding, allowing for some variance in sound.

Percussion – Percussion instruments have perhaps the most simple classification of instrument families. If an instrument is struck to make it’s sound, it is a percussion instrument. Due to the similarities between some percussion instruments, many musicians learn to use multiple kinds of percussion instruments as they can easily translate their initial skills. Sounds created are influenced by how hard the instrument is struck, as well as other factors such as such as the location hit, and in the case of drums, how tight the head of barrel-like percussion instruments are.

Woodwind – Woodwind instruments are similar to Brass instruments in that they involve the users mouth. However, Woodwind instruments focus less on the actual instruments structure and more on the path the air blown in must travel. Woodwind musicians are constantly covering and uncovering multiple holes adorning their instruments, which alters the pitch and tone of the sound produced. While not anymore, Woodwind instruments used to all be made from wood. This musical family has nonetheless kept the title despite most modern Woodwind instruments being made from a combination of plastic, wood or metal.

Community Benefits

The Barrier Reef Orchestra is a community driven organisation. Our dedicated members volunteer their time and talent to practice each performance for you to see. We each believe in doing our part to keep this amazing part of our culture blooming, as music is one of the universal languages we share as people.

It allows us to share and understand feelings in a way that words cannot do alone. It is our goal to inspire as many music lovers as we can to pursue their ambitions in this diverse cultural legacy whether they be young or old.

To that end, the Barrier Reef Orchestra is always willing to assist aspiring musicians. Solo artists can have difficulties in the beginning phases of their careers, as they lack the recognition needed to secure a stable position. We assist in this by allowing Solo artists to perform as guests in our performances. These performances allow the artists to showcase their skills, making a mark in this cultural field. Our performances only guests are not these aspiring artists, however. On occasion, we are also joined by famous conductors such as Richard McIntyre and Mario Dobernig.
