Conductors

The Barrier Reef Orchestra was first created under the talented baton of Richard McIntyre , and as such, he is generally considered to be our orchestra's main Conductor. Despite this, we enjoy engaging the rest of the international music community and regularly invite other talented Conductors as Guests to lead our major events. In commemoration of these magical nights, the following is a list of conductors who performed with us throughout the years.

Craig Johnson


Lieutenant Colonel Craig Johnson has lived his whole life with music. With many awards and achievements under his belt, Craig is a very prominent conductor on the world stage. He acted as a Guest Conductor to our orchestra in our 2016 show, ‘Barrier Reef Orchestra goes to the Opera’.

David Lawrence


A Guest Conductor who performed with our orchestra in the 2011 show, ‘Beethoven 9th Symphony’.

Mario Dobernig

Mark Shiell


Despite currently working as Artistic Director and Principal Conductor of three different orchestras, Mark also finds time to work as a guest teacher for Melbourne Conservatorium’s Master of Conducting course. He has appeared in numerous of our orchestra’s shows as a Guest Conductor.

Raymond Yong

Artists

While the majority of our orchestra is composed of local talented musicians who dedicate their own time to making our performances possible, they are also joined on stage by Guest Artists who work together with them to bring a new element to our ensemble. The following is a combined list of our local and guest musicians.

Adam Lopez


Adam Lopez is a man of exceptional vocal artistry. He has worked on backing vocals for many big names in the music industry. Adam joined our orchestra as a Guest conductor in our 2016 show ‘Barrier Reef Orchestra goes to the Opera’.

Alex Thomas


Alex Thomas is a long-standing musician of our orchestra. He has achieved numerous times the Champion of Champions and Most Pleasing Voice award while singing at the North Queensland Eisteddfod. Perfomances include Marius and Jean Valjean in “Les Miserables”, Dr Jekyll in “Jekyll and Hyde”, and Emile de Becque in “South Pacific”.
